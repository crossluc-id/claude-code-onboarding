# Research Profile

## About You

- **Name:** Atila Kovacs
- **Background:** I'm a session hairstylist based in Budapest with 12 years of cutting experience. About 2 years ago I started noticing that the best cuts I do follow mathematical patterns — not just the golden ratio stuff everyone talks about, but deeper structural relationships. I've been developing what I call Geometric Sectioning Theory (GST), a framework that applies concepts from topology, fractal geometry, and harmonic proportions to the physical act of haircutting. I teach workshops where stylists learn to "see" these patterns in hair and use them to make better creative decisions.
- **Technical level:** Beginner with code, strong in spatial mathematics and creative/visual research. I can read equations and proofs but I've never programmed. I use pen-and-paper diagrams, physical models, and lately some GeoGebra for visualization. I want Claude to handle the technical side while I focus on the conceptual framework.
- **Goals:**
  - Cross-reference GST with existing mathematical models in art, architecture, and nature (especially D'Arcy Thompson's "On Growth and Form" and the work on phyllotaxis)
  - Find if anyone else has applied topology or fractal mathematics to haircutting specifically
  - Explore publication formats: academic paper (maybe for a journal like Leonardo), blog series, interactive web essay, or a hybrid
  - Design a 6-session workshop series that teaches GST to working stylists with no math background
  - Build a website that showcases the framework with interactive visualizations
  - Eventually create an agent-based learning system where Claude helps stylists practice applying GST to photos of haircuts
